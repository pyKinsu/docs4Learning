## What is C?

C is a general-purpose, procedural programming language developed in the early 1970s by Dennis Ritchie at Bell Labs. It remains one of the most widely used languages in the world — powering operating systems, embedded systems, compilers, and performance-critical software.

<Info>
  C is often called the "mother of all languages." Languages like C++, Java, Python, and Go all trace major design decisions back to C.
</Info>

## Why learn C?

- **Understand how computers actually work** — memory, pointers, and system calls are not abstracted away.
- **Write highly performant software** — C gives you direct control over hardware resources.
- **Strong foundation** — once you know C, picking up any other language becomes significantly easier.
- **Widely used in systems programming** — Linux kernel, SQLite, CPython, and most embedded firmware are written in C.
